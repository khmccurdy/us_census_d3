U.S. Demographic and Health Correlations

Data collected from 2014 U.S. census surveys

Notable correlations include High school completion vs. Health, Disability employment vs. Costly doctor visits, White-collar jobs vs. Obesity, and Poverty vs. Health. Overall, data correlates as expected, with more high school completion, less poverty, greater disability employment, and more white-collar jobs tending toward better health, less financial strife with respect to doctors, and lower obesity rates.
